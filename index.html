KITE Research Institute, University Health Network (UHN)

Featured Project: Spatially Distributed Sequential Stimulation(SDSS) Hardware Adapter Design

Leading the development of a breakthrough Spatially Distributed Sequential Stimulation (SDSS) Hardware Adapter that revolutionizes Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES) therapy. This innovative system solves a critical problem in rehabilitation by intelligently distributing stimulation pulses across multiple electrodes, dramatically reducing muscle fatigue and extending therapy sessions.

Technical Implementation: Designed sophisticated pulse detection circuitry using voltage dividers, clamping diodes, and signal amplifiers. Developed real-time STM32 firmware with external interrupt handling for precise timing control. Selected high-voltage analog switch for output routing and implemented pulse detection and channel cycling logic in C. Completed full schematic in Altium Designer and currently working on PCB layout with careful isolation of analog/digital domains and high-voltage safety considerations.

Impact: This system addresses the fundamental limitation of standard FES devices that repeatedly stimulate the same muscle group, causing premature fatigue. By sequentially distributing pulses across multiple electrodes, it simulates more natural muscle activation patterns, significantly improving rehabilitation outcomes for patients with spinal cord injuries or neurological conditions.
